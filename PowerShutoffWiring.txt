===================================================================
      ROBOT SAFETY CIRCUIT: SOLENOID & E-STOP WIRING LAYOUT
====================================================================

[SYSTEM OVERVIEW]
This layout separates low-current control logic (E-Stop and Coil) 
from the high-current power distribution (Robot Load). The fuses 
safeguard both lines, the diode dampens coil collapse spikes, 
and the resistor acts as a snubber across the heavy power contacts.

====================================================================
                      CRITICAL SAFETY CHECKLIST
====================================================================
[ ] DIODE ORIENTATION: 
    The stripe (cathode) faces the incoming positive wire from the 
    E-Stop. The non-stripe side (anode) faces the ground line. 
    If flipped backward, flipping the circuit on will instantly blow 
    your control fuse.

[ ] E-STOP TERMINALS: 
    Ensure you use the NC (Normally Closed) blocks on your E-Stop. 
    Hitting the physical button breaks this connection mechanically, 
    guaranteeing power drop even if electronic logic fails.

[ ] FUSE PROXIMITY: 
    Install both the Control Fuse and Power Fuse as physically close 
    to the battery positive terminal as possible to minimize unfused, 
    vulnerable wiring.
